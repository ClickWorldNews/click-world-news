How pricing works (simple)

1) Build fee (one-time)

You pay once for setup and launch. This covers planning, implementation, testing, and go-live.

2) Monthly Ops

You pay monthly so ongoing model/API usage, updates, and support are covered.

3) Usage tiers

More usage (messages, tasks, workflows) = higher tier. That prevents surprise losses.

Example: costs depend on usage. A light-use client might cost far less than $200/month on pay-as-you-go, while heavier usage can cost more. Price monthly Ops with a base fee + usage tiers so margin stays healthy at both low and high usage.

Pricing + scope notice

  • Build fees cover the agreed initial scope and launch deliverables.
  • Monthly Ops covers maintenance within your selected usage tier.
  • Higher usage, added integrations, or new workflows may require plan upgrades or a separate quote.
  • Third-party platform/API fees are billed to client directly or passed through at cost.
  • All scope changes require written approval before implementation.
